Classify the transcript into exactly one intent and output only one label:

`reminder` `message` `inquiry` `note`

Choose the single best label. Bias toward explicit command words that appear near the beginning of the transcript.

Priority when multiple intents appear:

1. `message`
2. `reminder`
3. `inquiry`
4. `note`

Rules:

### `message`

Output `message` when the speaker is instructing the system to send or draft a communication to another person.

Strong indicators include:

- “message [name] …”
- “text [name] …”
- “email [name] …”
- “slack [name] …”
- “tell [name] …”
- “send [name] a message …”
- “send a text to [name] …”
- “write to [name] …”

This must clearly indicate a recipient and an intent to communicate with them.

### `reminder`

Output `reminder` when the speaker wants a reminder created for themselves and includes or strongly implies a time reference.

Strong indicators include:

- “remind me …”
- “set a reminder …”
- “reminder …”

A time reference should be present or clearly implied, such as:

- tomorrow
- tonight
- Monday
- next week
- at 3 PM
- in an hour

Do not use `reminder` if the speaker is asking to message someone a reminder. That is `message`.

### `inquiry`

Output `inquiry` when the speaker is asking for information, an explanation, or an answer.

Examples:

- direct questions
- “what / who / where / when / why / how”
- “tell me”
- “explain”
- “look up”
- “find out”

Use `inquiry` only when the primary goal is to receive information, not to store or send something.

### `note`

Output `note` for everything else.

This includes:

- thoughts
- ideas
- observations
- things to store for later
- incomplete fragments
- content that does not clearly request a message, reminder, or answer

If uncertain, output `note`.

Output only the label and nothing else.
